Worth knowing early

Pay yourself first

Move money to savings the day you're paid, before anything else — treat it like a bill. Even $50/month builds the habit; the amount can grow later.

Emergency fund before extras

A starter cushion of $1,000, then work toward 3 months of committed bills. With a growing family, this fund is what turns a crisis into an inconvenience.

Financing is renting money

A payment plan always costs more than the sticker price — the calculator shows exactly how much more. If you can wait and save, you keep that difference.

Watch the monthly creep

Subscriptions, upgrades, and “only $30/month” add-ons stack quietly. Once a quarter, list every recurring charge and cancel what you don't actually use.

When kids arrive, check the paperwork

Add them to health insurance quickly (there's a deadline after birth), name beneficiaries, and consider basic term life insurance while it's cheap.

Talk money monthly

A 20-minute money date with your spouse each month — what came in, what went out, what's next — prevents most money fights before they start.
